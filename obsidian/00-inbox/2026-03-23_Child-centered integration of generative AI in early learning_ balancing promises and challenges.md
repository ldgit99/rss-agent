## Abstract
Abstract Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) is rapidly transforming educational contexts, yet its role in early childhood education (ECE) raises complex issues regarding developmental appropriateness, ethics, and equity. ECE deserves special attention because it represents a uniquely sensitive and formative stage of human development. This conceptual paper examines the promises and challenges of integrating GenAI into early learning environments, emphasizing both opportunities for innovation and the need for critical safeguards. The discussion highlights potential benefits, including personalized and adaptive learning, enhanced multimodal storytelling, support for play-based pedagogy, and teacher workload reduction. These opportunities, however, are moderated by challenges and risks such as overreliance on screen-based interactions, displacement of play and social relationships, inequitable access, algorithmic bias, and threats to children’s privacy and safety. To address these tensions, the paper situates GenAI within developmental psychology, sociocultural learning theory, educational technology frameworks, and ethical paradigms. Policy and practice implications are outlined, stressing the importance of regulation, equity-focused infrastructure, teacher professional development, and culturally responsive curriculum design. The paper concludes with recommendations for educators, policymakers, and researchers, and introduces a conceptual framework for “Child-Centered GenAI Integration,” arguing that developmental appropriateness, ethical safeguards, and sociocultural mediation must work together to ensure child-centered GenAI use in ECE.

# Summary
이 논문은 생성적 인공지능(GenAI)이 조기 아동 교육(ECE)에서의 역할과 그로 인해 발생하는 복잡한 문제들을 다룬다. GenAI는 개인화된 학습, 다중 모드 스토리텔링, 놀이 기반 교수법 지원, 교사의 업무 부담 경감 등의 기회를 제공하지만, 화면 기반 상호작용에 대한 과도한 의존, 놀이와 사회적 관계의 대체, 불공정한 접근, 알고리즘 편향, 아동의 프라이버시 및 안전에 대한 위협과 같은 도전과 위험도 동반한다. 이 논문은 GenAI를 발달 심리학 및 사회문화적 학습 이론과 연결하여, 정책 및 실천적 함의를 제시하며, 아동 중심의 GenAI 통합을 위한 개념적 프레임워크를 제안한다.
