## Abstract
The rapid advancement of digital technologies, data-driven learning systems, and cognitive sciences has radically reshaped the landscape of mathematics education, prompting a paradigm shift from traditional, teacher-cantered methods to dynamic, student-cantered learning ecosystems. Next-Generation Mathematics Education (NGME) represents an emerging educational framework that integrates artificial intelligence, personalized adaptive learning pathways, interdisciplinary problem-solving approaches, competency-based assessment models, and culturally responsive pedagogy rooted in both global and local knowledge systems. In this evolving environment, AI-driven platforms and intelligent tutoring systems enable individualized instruction, real-time feedback, and mastery-based progression, while digital simulations, mathematical visualization tools, and coding-integrated tasks deepen conceptual understanding and enhance learner engagement. Cognitive science research further contributes by offering insights into how students construct mathematical meaning, retain problem-solving strategies, and overcome math anxiety. The convergence of these technological and scientific developments broadens the scope of mathematical literacy to include data reasoning, algorithmic thinking, modeling capabilities, and creative exploration. This paper critically examines the emerging paradigms, innovative tools, and pedagogical practices that are shaping the future of mathematics teaching and learning at school and higher education levels. It highlights the major gaps in current instructional practices such as rote memorization, procedural teaching, lack of personalization, minimal use of digital tools, and insufficient real-world applicability and analyses how NGME can effectively bridge these gaps. Furthermore, the paper proposes a comprehensive, future-oriented framework integrating technology, cognitive research, and culturally relevant knowledge to foster deeper understanding, equity, and long-term learner success. Finally, the study offers practical suggestions and policy recommendations aimed at transforming mathematics education into a more engaging, inclusive, equitable, and future-ready discipline. By doing so, it emphasizes the urgent need for educational reforms, teacher capacity-building, and systemic support to fully realize the potential of next-generation mathematics education.
